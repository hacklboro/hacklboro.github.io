What is a Hackathon?

A Hackathon is a collaborative, creative event where teams will develop software or hardware solutions to fix a set of problems set up by the event organizers. The event will last for 24h and it will put its atendees through a 'getting-work-done' environemnt which will be challenging yet rewarding.

Who attends a Hackathon?

There are three clear stakeholders in a Hackathon. The organizing body (Hackathon committee, MLH, the SU, ...), the atendees (computer science students, design students, ...) and the sponsors. Sponsoring is a big part of making the event happen.

What is MLH?

Major League Hacking works with independently run university hackathons and provides them with various benefits such as the MLH Hardware Lab, Code of Conduct, eligibility for Season Rankings and Event Support.[8] At the end of every season, it formulates a winner of the League based on participation and prizes and organizes an awards ceremony at their school where the MLH Hacker Cup is provided to the school.

How does sponsoring help?

A sponsor can have a big say in what goes on in the event; things like choosing prize categories. Apart from that the sponsors are also important in the experience of the event - as 24h gives plenty of time to run anything from workshops to short meetings to discuss placement opportunities.

Why HackLboro?

In short; there are two trends and one fact. Fact One: Loughborough University has never held a 24h student-run hackathon event. Trend One: More and more university are starting to run Hackathons and Fact Two: Loughborough has been steadily increasing in both global and subject-specific leaderboards - this translates to a large pool of students who could greatly benefit from an event like this.
